# §1 Introduction

*Target length: 0.75 page of 6-page ACM double-column (~450 words
prose). Word-count below: ~450.*

---

Large language models (LLMs) have rapidly moved from passive explainers
of analog circuits to active participants in the design loop. Recent
systems delegate transistor sizing, topology selection, or netlist
generation to an LLM that talks to a SPICE simulator and revises its
proposal each iteration [ADO-LLM, AnalogCoder, EEsizer, AnaFlow,
LEDRO, AutoSizer, AnalogSAGE]. The reported success rates on benchmarks
such as AnalogGym are encouraging, and the systems-paper formula is
now clear: parameterized testbench, spec-driven prompt, closed loop,
ablation across LLM choices.

Almost every published demonstration runs against an **open process
design kit (PDK)** — Skywater 130, FreePDK 45, or PTM-MG predictive
models. Open PDKs are an indispensable research instrument; they are
a poor proxy for industrial deployment. A real analog team works inside
an NDA boundary that forbids transmitting *any* of the following to a
commercial LLM endpoint: BSIM4 / BSIM-CMG parameter sets, foundry cell
names, the process stack description, and the proprietary cell
library's topology. When the loop is closed against a real foundry
kit, the spec evaluator, the schematic reader, and the op-point dump
all see those bytes — and the loop must guarantee none of them cross
the trust boundary back to the LLM.

Concurrent work AnalogAgent [2026] addresses the same constraint
*orthogonally*: it restricts the LLM to tiny on-premise open-weight
models (Qwen3 1.7B-14B), trading off LLM capability for full
deployment isolation. We take the complementary path — admit any
cloud LLM, and prevent foundry content from ever reaching it via a
filter layer. The two stories are not in conflict; they bracket the
deployment design space.

We present **Safe-Bridge**, the trust-boundary topology that lets a
closed-loop analog optimization agent drive a real industrial-node
PDK while satisfying the foundry NDA. Built on the
PC ↔ remote-host SKILL IPC channel (`virtuoso-bridge-lite`),
Safe-Bridge inserts four defense layers between the LLM and the
foundry content: a Python parameter-name whitelist, a 17-name SKILL
entrypoint allow-list on the Cadence side, a return-path scrubber that
strips foundry tokens and absolute paths, and a reasoning-trace scrub
that closes the chain-of-thought replay channel for Kimi K2.5,
MiniMax M2.7, and reasoning-capable Ollama models.

Our contributions:

- **SP1.** Safe-Bridge architecture (§3, §4) — an
  **industrial-grade NDA-safe closed-loop** for analog sizing with
  **four verifiable scrub layers** between any cloud LLM and the
  foundry content. Concurrent AnalogAgent [2026] addresses the same
  constraint via on-prem tiny models; our path admits any cloud LLM
  and audits non-egress at code-anchored invariants (§4).
- **SP2.** Failure-mode taxonomy (§5) over N runs × 4 projects × 5
  LLMs, classified into 5-7 buckets reported per-provider.
- **SP3.** Cost Pareto (§6) — per-iteration token cost with separate
  reasoning-token accounting, against spec-pass rate.
- **SP4.** Open-LLM eval (§7) — Kimi K2.5, MiniMax M2.7, Ollama as
  industrially deployable alternatives. To our knowledge no prior
  work in this space evaluates a domestic-China LLM.
