\begin{abstract}
We present the first NDA-safe, industrial-PDK, eleven-LLM analog
sizing benchmark in ACM TODAES, extending prior LLM4EDA work in the
digital and high-level-synthesis domains (AutoChip~\cite{autochip},
HLSRewriter~\cite{hlsrewriter}, C2HLSC~\cite{c2hlsc},
LHS~\cite{lhs}, ConfiBench~\cite{confibench}) into the analog /
SPICE-loop domain under realistic foundry-PDK confidentiality
constraints; concurrent TODAES analog work
AMSnet-KG~\cite{amsnetkg} targets open-PDK netlist auto-design via
knowledge-graph RAG and is orthogonal to our scrubbed-cloud-LLM
deployment substrate. We introduce \emph{Safe-Bridge}, a
verifiable NDA-safe substrate that lets any cloud LLM drive a real
foundry-bound schematic without PDK content crossing the trust
boundary, and use it to run an eleven-checkpoint industrial-node
benchmark spanning seven vendor families across two countries
(6~US + 5~China). We report a cost--quality Pareto across the
checkpoints, a labelled \emph{reasoning-token premium} sharp
observation, and an eight-bucket failure-mode taxonomy. The
methodology contribution---multi-LLM benchmark on a closed
industrial PDK with three-axis scrub-audit rigor---is documented
in a dedicated reproducibility section as a reusable pattern for
future analog (and adjacent EDA) LLM4EDA evaluations.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{LLM agents, analog circuit sizing, EDA benchmarks,
