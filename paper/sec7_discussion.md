## 7.1  The viability question, framed cross-vendor

Can an industrial analog-sizing flow depend on domestic-China LLM
inference? Prior work cannot answer this; AnalogAgent [Bao et al.,
2026] takes the on-prem-tiny route, leaving cloud-frontier viability
open. Our four-vendor China-frontier cohort lets us answer in three
dimensions — quality, cost, and operational maturity — and crucially
to detect whether viability is a *cohort pattern* or *vendor-
specific*.

## 7.2  Quality

*Filled at D8.* Anticipated framing: across the four China-frontier
checkpoints, median success rate on LC_VCO_20G is within ε% of the
US-frontier-3 (Opus 4.7, GPT-5.5, Gemini 2.x), with ε = TBD-from-D6.
The cross-vendor spread within China (TBD-from-D6 percentage points)
is the key indicator: a narrow spread argues the pattern is robust;
a wide spread argues that picking a specific China vendor matters
more than picking US-vs-China. Either outcome is publishable.

## 7.3  Cost

*Filled at D8.* Anticipated framing: under the 2026-05-01 public
pricing snapshot (appendix), the China-frontier cohort lists at
TBD-from-D6× lower per-output-token price than the US frontier;
whether this list-price advantage survives the per-iteration
reasoning-token premium (§5.5) to yield a lower *per-run* cost
ordering — and in particular whether DeepSeek V4-flash and MiMo
v2.5-pro fall below GPT-5.4-mini / Haiku 4.5 on per-run USD — is
the open empirical question D8 answers. The cost-quality story for
practitioners reduces to whether ε-loss on quality (§7.2) is
acceptable for the cost saving — answered as a per-budget operating
point in §5.6.

## 7.4  Operational maturity

Three concerns turn up in any LLM rollout against a real workload:
**rate-limit / 429 behaviour**, **reasoning-token accounting
reliability**, and **stability of the public API surface** across a
revision pair (V4-pro vs V4-flash; Sonnet 4.6 vs Haiku 4.5). The §6
bucket matrix surfaces rate-limit asymmetry per LLM; §5.5 surfaces
reasoning-token accounting; API-surface stability we report
qualitatively per vendor in the appendix. The MiMo v2.5-pro entry
is the most uncertain on this axis (newest entrant; client
implementation assumes OpenAI-compatible shape, live smoke
verification pending mlcad_runner D5); DeepSeek's MoE routing may
introduce an additional latency-tail concern that we track
separately from generic cloud-endpoint latency-tail sources
(batching / network / rate limits).

## 7.5  Take-aways

- **For practitioners.** The Pareto recommendation reduces to a
  per-budget table (§5.6): for token-budget operating points where
  the China-frontier cohort is within ε of US-frontier, the cost
  saving justifies the choice; below that threshold, the US-
  frontier remains preferred. Operational caveats per §7.4 should
  feed the procurement risk decision.
- **For researchers.** The reusable methodology contribution is
  **multi-LLM benchmark on an industrial PDK** — the platform (§3)
  + the protocol (§4.4 seed-handling, §4.6 three-axis audit) + the
  cost-quality + failure-bucket reporting (§5/§6) are reusable for
  future analog (and adjacent EDA) LLM evaluations. The 11×spec-
  contract matrix design is the contribution; specific numbers will
  age, but the matrix shape will not.
