## 8.1  Conclusion

We presented an **eleven-LLM benchmark for industrial-node analog
sizing** running on **Safe-Bridge**, a verifiable NDA-safe substrate
that lets any cloud LLM drive a real foundry-bound schematic without
PDK content crossing the trust boundary. The eleven checkpoints
span **seven vendor families across two countries (6 US + 5 China)**
with a **four-vendor China-frontier cohort** that turns SP4 from a
single-vendor anecdote into a cross-vendor pattern. The cost-quality Pareto
(§5) with its **reasoning-token premium** sharp evidence and the
failure-bucket matrix (§6) constitute the reusable contribution for
future industrial-EDA LLM evaluations.

## 8.2  Future work

- **Multi-circuit expansion.** The hand-built OpAmp testbench
  reported as the §1/§4 in-revision hook; further targets include a
  comparator and a bandgap reference to broaden the benchmark
  beyond a single circuit.
- **Multi-PDK / multi-node expansion.** All eleven checkpoints are
  evaluated on a single industrial node; cross-foundry and cross-
  node generalisation (different BSIM-CMG generations, different
  cell libraries, different proprietary process stacks) is the
  obvious next-axis benchmark. Safe-Bridge's PDK-agnostic scrub
  layers (`_FOUNDRY_LEAK_RE` is configurable per project) make the
  platform side trivially portable; the spec-contract side needs
  per-PDK metric-range recalibration.
- **Gemini family expansion** and an **Anthropic / Gemini reasoning-
  token accounting schema extension** (capturing
  `cache_creation_input_tokens` / `cache_read_input_tokens` and the
  Gemini `thinking` block) so the §5.5 premium row currently marked
  unavailable can be filled.
- **Tier-3 first-emission closure.** Closing the provider-side
  reasoning-trace capture (§3.5 out-of-scope row) requires either a
  hash-only thinking channel or a fully on-prem inference stack
  (Ollama path); the latter is a natural point of comparison with
  AnalogAgent [Bao et al., 2026].
- **ABNF / JSON-Schema tightening on `design_vars`** targeting the
  contract-violation bucket pre-registered in §6.
