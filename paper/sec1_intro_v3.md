---

Large language models are now active participants in analog sizing
loops, calling SPICE simulators and revising netlists across
iterations [AnalogCoder, ADO-LLM, EEsizer, AnaFlow, LEDRO, AutoSizer,
AnalogSAGE]. Reported success rates on open-PDK benchmarks such as
AnalogGym are encouraging, and EEsizer [NEWCAS 2025] has begun to
look at multi-LLM evaluation on six basic circuits. Two practical
questions, however, remain unanswered as a *conjunction*: **(i)
which LLM should a team pick under a token budget when running
against a real industrial PDK?** — no prior work pairs multi-LLM
evaluation with industrial-node deployment, cost-quality Pareto
analysis, *and* per-LLM failure-mode taxonomy; and **(ii) is
domestic-China LLM inference industrially viable for analog sizing?**
— to our knowledge zero published work evaluates Moonshot, MiniMax,
Xiaomi, or DeepSeek on this workload.

Concurrent work AnalogAgent [Bao et al., 2026] addresses a related
NDA constraint *orthogonally* via on-premise tiny open-weight models
(Qwen3 1.7B–14B); we address the deployment-economics constraint
via an eleven-checkpoint cost–quality Pareto on a real industrial
PDK. The two stories bracket the industrial-deployment design space.

We present an **eleven-LLM benchmark for industrial-node analog
sizing** running on **Safe-Bridge**, a verifiable NDA-safe wrapper
around the PC↔remote-host SKILL IPC channel that ensures no foundry
content reaches any cloud LLM. The benchmark covers **seven vendor
families across two countries (6 US + 5 China)** — Anthropic Opus
4.7 / Sonnet 4.6 / Haiku 4.5, OpenAI GPT-5.5 / GPT-5.4-mini, Google
Gemini 2.x, Moonshot Kimi K2.5, MiniMax M2.7, Xiaomi MiMo v2.5-pro,
and DeepSeek V4-pro / V4-flash. We evaluate on LC_VCO_20GHz; an
additional OpAmp testbench will be reported in revision/appendix if
available before the submission deadline. The four-vendor China-
frontier cohort (Moonshot, MiniMax, Xiaomi, DeepSeek) turns SP4 from
a single anecdote into a cross-vendor pattern.
