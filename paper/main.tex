\begin{abstract}
We present an eleven-LLM benchmark for industrial-node analog sizing
running on \emph{Safe-Bridge}, a verifiable NDA-safe substrate that
lets any cloud LLM drive a real foundry-bound schematic without PDK
content crossing the trust boundary. The eleven checkpoints span
seven vendor families across two countries (6~US + 5~China) with a
four-vendor China-frontier cohort that turns cross-vendor
domestic-China viability from a single-vendor anecdote into a
cross-vendor pattern. We report a cost--quality Pareto across the
checkpoints, a labelled \emph{reasoning-token premium} sharp
observation, and an eight-bucket failure-mode taxonomy. The
methodology contribution---multi-LLM benchmark on a closed
industrial PDK with three-axis scrub-audit rigor---is reusable for
future analog (and adjacent EDA) LLM evaluations.
\end{abstract}
